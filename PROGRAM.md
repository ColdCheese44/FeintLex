# The FeintLex Protocol

A research-backed Spanish acquisition program for a **reading/writing-first learner**, built to run inside FeintLex. Every rule below is grounded in second-language-acquisition research; every mission maps to a FeintLex feature.

## Why this program works

### 1. Balanced strands, weighted for how you learn

Paul Nation's **Four Strands** model — the most widely adopted evidence-based curriculum framework in L2 teaching — splits learning time across meaning-focused input, meaning-focused output, language-focused learning, and fluency development ([Nation interview, 2025](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/390957554_Vocabulary_Within_a_Four_Strands_Curriculum_An_Interview_with_Paul_Nation); [The Four Strands](https://www.academia.edu/41764855/The_Four_Strands)). Nation prescribes 25% each; FeintLex reweights toward your strengths while keeping every strand alive:

| Strand | Standard | FeintLex Protocol | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Meaning-focused input (reading) | 25% | **40%** | You learn best through reading; extensive reading transfers to every other skill |
| Meaning-focused output (writing) | 25% | **25%** | Writing with corrective feedback has a proven moderate-to-strong effect |
| Language-focused learning (SRS, grammar, autopsy) | 25% | **25%** | Deliberate study is the highest-ROI activity below ~3,000 words |
| Fluency development (reread, listen) | 25% | **10%** | Kept minimal but never zero — listening drills stay on the menu |

### 2. Frequency-first vocabulary

Text comprehension requires knowing **95–98% of the running words** ([Nation 2006 coverage threshold](https://www.lingref.com/cpp/hls/7/paper1091.pdf); [graded reader guide](https://eppika.com/en/blog/spanish-graded-readers-guide)). Roughly **3,000 word families ≈ 95% coverage** of ordinary Spanish text. The fastest route there is frequency order: [Davies' Frequency Dictionary of Spanish](https://www.amazon.com/Frequency-Dictionary-Spanish-Vocabulary-Dictionaries/dp/0415334292) (20M-word corpus) and the [Wiktionary Spanish frequency lists](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Wiktionary:Frequency_lists/Spanish) show the first 1,000 words carry the overwhelming bulk of coverage. FeintLex's Core Signal decks are built in frequency order for exactly this reason.

### 3. Extensive reading is your engine

A 2025 meta-analysis of 74 studies found extensive reading improves **every** domain — vocabulary, comprehension, fluency, writing, even speaking — with effect size d = 0.57 ([summary](https://eppika.com/en/blog/spanish-graded-readers-guide); [Extensive reading overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extensive_reading)). Incidental vocabulary research shows a word needs **~8 or more encounters in context** before form and meaning stick ([Brown, Waring & Donkaewbua 2008](https://www2.hawaii.edu/~readfl/rfl/October2008/brown/brown.html)) — which is why the Protocol has you importing real texts daily and why FeintLex counts term encounters.

### 4. Writing with direct, focused correction

Meta-analyses of written corrective feedback show a **moderate positive effect on grammatical accuracy**, with **direct feedback** (showing the fix) and **focused/selective feedback** (targeting error patterns, not everything) outperforming alternatives — especially for lower-proficiency learners ([Kang & Han 2015, Modern Language Journal](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/modl.12189); [TESL-EJ 2020 meta-analysis](https://tesl-ej.org/wordpress/issues/volume24/ej95/ej95a3/); [direct vs indirect study](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10830576/)). FeintLex's writing coach gives direct, pattern-focused corrections and files every error into the mistake bank for spaced review.

### 5. Spaced retrieval, not rereading

Spaced practice has a **medium-to-large effect** on L2 learning; longer gaps beat short gaps for long-term retention, and expanding vs equal intervals perform about the same — what matters is *retrieval from memory at increasing delays* ([L2 spacing meta-analysis](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/358406370_The_Effects_of_Spaced_Practice_on_Second_Language_Learning_A_Meta-Analysis); [spacing meta-analytic review](http://www.lscp.net/persons/ramus/docs/EPR20.pdf); [overview](https://www.justinmath.com/cognitive-science-of-learning-spaced-repetition/)). The Signal Queue and mistake bank run on expanding intervals (1 → 3 → 7 → 14 → 30 days). Always answer **from memory before revealing** — that's the testing effect doing the work.

### 6. Sentence mining: the 1T rule

From the [Refold immersion community](https://refold.la/roadmap/stage-2/a/basic-sentence-mining/): mine **"1T sentences"** — sentences from your reading with exactly **one** unknown word. Skip sentences with 3+ unknowns and words you'll never meet again ([sentence mining library](https://refold.la/roadmap/library/sentence-mining)). Long-term Spanish case studies confirm reading-heavy immersion plus SRS compounds dramatically over ~1,000 hours ([1100-hour update](https://deusexvita.medium.com/refold-approach-to-language-learning-spanish-1100-hour-update-ac3bb7712009)).

## The Daily Protocol (60 minutes)

Run this 6 days a week. The dashboard's **Daily Protocol** panel tracks it live.

| # | Mission | Strand | Time | FeintLex feature |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Clear the Signal Queue** — answer from memory, then grade honestly | Study | 10 min | Signal Queue panel |
| 2 | **Import + read one Spanish text** (news, match report, subtitles — 150+ words) | Input | 20 min | Source Intake → lesson |
| 3 | **Autopsy 2 sentences** from the text — isolate verbs, connectors, pattern; mine 1T sentences | Study | 10 min | Sentence Autopsy |
| 4 | **Write 5+ sentences** reacting to the text; submit for correction; read every fix | Output | 15 min | Writing Practice |
| 5 | **Drill weak signals** — flashcards, drill tab, or ask the coach "quiz me" | Study | 5 min | Drill / Coach |
| 6 | **One listening pass** (optional but keep the ear alive) | Fluency | 5 min | Listen tab |

**Weekly rhythm:** 6 protocol days + 1 light day (only clear the queue + reread old lesson exports for speed — fluency work uses *easy, familiar* material).

## Writing SOP (what to write each day)

Rotate through the week — reacting to what you read that day:
1. **Summary** — 5 sentences summarizing the text (uses: past tenses, connectors)
2. **Opinion** — agree/disagree and why (uses: creer que, porque, aunque)
3. **Interrogation** — 5 questions you'd ask the article's subject (uses: question formation)
4. **Prediction** — what happens next (uses: future, ir a + infinitive)
5. **Retelling** — the same story from another actor's viewpoint (uses: person/tense shifts)
6. **Free write** — anything, but use 3 terms from your weak-signal list

After correction: **rewrite the passage once clean**, from memory. One rewrite, no more (diminishing returns).

## Phases and milestones

Phase advancement is automatic — the Daily Protocol panel reads your live stats.

### Phase 1 — Foundation (0 → ~300 known signals)
- Lock all Core Signal + scenario decks to level 3+
- Read: FeintLex lesson texts, ultra-short news blurbs
- Write: present tense, 5-sentence summaries
- Exit: 300 known signals, conjugate ser/estar/tener/ir cold

### Phase 2 — Signal Expansion (~300 → 1,000)
- Add graded readers (A2/B1) at ~98% coverage — one per week if possible
- Read: short news articles (BBC Mundo, 20minutos), soccer match reports
- Write: past-tense retellings; start the interrogation drills
- Exit: 1,000 known signals; read an A2 graded reader without a dictionary

### Phase 3 — Field Immersion (~1,000 → 3,000)
- Read: full news articles, subtitle files, cybersecurity blogs in Spanish
- Mine 1T sentences aggressively; autopsy anything that resists you
- Write: opinion paragraphs, incident-report style summaries
- Exit: 3,000 known signals ≈ 95% coverage of ordinary text

### Phase 4 — Native Operations (3,000+)
- Read: novels, long-form journalism, technical documents
- Write: multi-paragraph analysis; correction should be finding few errors
- The queue becomes maintenance; reading volume is the whole game

## Non-negotiables

1. **Retrieve before revealing.** Never flip a card or show an answer before attempting from memory.
2. **Grade honestly.** "Missed" is data, not failure — it reschedules the item where it's needed.
3. **95%+ comprehension reading.** If a text has 5+ unknown words per sentence, it's too hard — autopsy one sentence and find an easier text.
4. **Volume beats intensity.** 60 focused minutes daily outperforms a 7-hour Saturday (spacing effect).
5. **Every error becomes a card.** The mistake bank is your personalized textbook.
